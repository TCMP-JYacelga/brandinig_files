About Standing Instructions (SI)

Standing Instructions are instructions that are initiated at one time for making multiple payments at stipulated frequency. You can define various frequencies for processing the standing instructions. Standing instructions are instructions that are processed in near future and not at the time of defining a standing instruction. However, the effective date and the termination date of the standing instruction must be within the start and end date of the frequency selected to execute the instruction.

This service facilitates the customer to instruct the bank to transfer funds at pre-set frequency / time and amounts from the customer's account to beneficiaries account and can be used to effect repayment of loans, salary payments, bill payments, etc.

The setup required for the Standing Instruction includes setting up the Frequency for executing the standing instruction. Rest of the fields are the same as the manual (Payment) transaction initiation mentioned earlier.

The Standing Instruction (SI) summary screen  displays a list of all the transactions initiated along with the relevant details and the status.

Initiating an SI Transaction:

1.      Click the in the menu bar. The New Payments screen opens showing a list of MyProducts defined for the client.

1    Select a MyProduct that you want to use for processing the current transaction.

The MyProducts are grouped under two heads- My Products and Bank Products.

Group of bank products clubbed together are displayed under MyProducts and are usually associated with -

□        Salary

□        Vendor Payments

□        Reimbursements

MyProduct having only one bank product are displayed under Bank Products like-

□        Book transfers

□        Hi-Value Payments

2    Entering Batch Details: For Multiple Instrument Transaction- Batch Pay, you will be directed to the Batch Header SI screen

3    Entering Instrument Details: For Single Instrument Transaction- Quick Pay , you will bypass the Batch Header and will be directed to the  Instrument Entry SI/Quick Pay SI Entry screen.

4    Submitting a Transaction:  On successful initiation, the transaction will appear in this screen with the status showing - Pending Submit. Select the record and click the  icon to make it available for authorization. If successfully submitted, system displays a message confirming it and notifies the Client Checker (authorizer) that the transaction has been entered and closed and is awaiting authorization.

§        In case Authorization Level field in My Product master is set to Batch level, you can Submit, Authorize and Send the transaction at Batch level.

§        In case Authorization Level field in My Product master is set to Instrument level, you can Submit, Authorize and Send the transaction at Instrument level.

§        In case of Quick Pay or where the Batch has only one instrument, actions such as Submit, Authorize and Send will always be at Instrument level.

Note: In case you are assigned Across Entity rights for a corporation, you can Submit, Verify, Authorize, Reject, Send, Stop, Hold and Release and Send transactions of clients spread across different countries. This feature is based on the settings maintained for the User- Client relationship in the Seller Corporation master maintained by the bank.

§        Based on the Maker-Checker concept, if you are eligible for authorization, the status of the record will show - Pending My Auth or Pending My Verification (if Verification Required flag is selected in the Payment MyProduct master).

Editing a Standing Instruction:

1.      Click Edit to go to the Batch Header SI screen in case you are at Batch level, or the Standing Instruction Instrument Edit screen if you are at Instrument level, where you can edit a previously initiated transaction.

2.      Click Update to save the changes.

Note:  You can modify a transaction only till such time that it is not Submitted.

Viewing a Standing Instruction:

1.      Click View to go to the Batch Header SI screen in case you are at Batch level, or the Standing Instruction Instrument View screen if you are at Instrument level,where you can view a previously initiated instructions. You cannot modify the same in this screen.

2.      Click Back to return to the Payments center.
